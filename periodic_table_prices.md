Report Date: August 13, 2025
This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the pricing and sourcing of Hydrogen (H), including its common isotopes Deuterium (D) and Tritium (T). It covers current and historical price data for the last 15 years, a breakdown of information sources, and an analysis of data consistency.

1. Hydrogen Price Analysis
The price of hydrogen is heavily dependent on its production method, purity, and delivery logistics. The main commercial forms are compressed gas and cryogenic liquid. Solid hydrogen is not a commercially traded product.

1.1. Current Pricing (2024-2025)
Gaseous Hydrogen:
- **Wholesale/Bulk Price (per kg):**
  - **Grey Hydrogen (from SMR):** ~$2.00/kg. Price is highly correlated with the cost of natural gas feedstock.
  - **Blue Hydrogen (SMR with CCS):** ~$4.00 - $5.00/kg. The cost of carbon capture and storage adds a significant premium.
  - **Green Hydrogen (from Electrolysis):** ~$4.00 - $6.00/kg. Price depends on the cost of renewable electricity and electrolyzer technology.
- **Retail/Lab Price (per gram):** ~$8.00 - $31.00/g. This price is for small, high-purity cylinders and includes significant markups for purification and handling.

Liquid Hydrogen (LH2):
- **Bulk/Wholesale Price:** No public price is available. LH2 is sold via long-term contracts, with a significant price premium over gaseous hydrogen due to the energy-intensive liquefaction process (which can add $1-2/kg to the cost) and specialized cryogenic handling.
- **Retail/Dewar Price:** Not typically sold in small retail quantities due to high boil-off rates and handling complexity.

2.1. Deuterium (D) Price Analysis
Deuterium is most commonly traded as deuterium gas (D2) or heavy water (D2O).
- **Current Price (2024-2025):**
  - **D2 Gas:** ~$50 - $90 per gram for high-purity gas in small cylinders.

Tritium is a rare, radioactive isotope critical for nuclear weapons, fusion research, and self-powered lighting. Its supply is tightly controlled, primarily by government entities.
- **Current Price (2024-2025):** ~$30,000 - $100,000 per gram. The price is extremely high and variable, depending on the buyer, purity, and geopolitical factors.
- **Historical Price:**
  - **1992:** Estimated production cost was $215 - $1,420 per gram.
  - **2000:** The market price was widely cited as ~$30,000 per gram.
  - **2022:** The market price was estimated to be in the $30,000 - $100,000 per gram range, showing a stable to increasing trend driven by fusion research demand and limited supply.

Helium (He) Pricing and Sourcing Analysis
Report Date: August 10, 2025
This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the pricing and sourcing of Helium (He), including its common isotope Helium-3 (3He). It covers current and historical price data for the last 15 years, a breakdown of information sources, and an analysis of data consistency.

1. Helium (4He) Price Analysis
Helium is unique in that it does not solidify at standard pressure, regardless of the temperature. Therefore, commercial pricing for solid helium is not available. The primary commercial forms are liquid and gas.
1.1. Current Pricing (2024-2025)
Gaseous Helium (Grade-A, 99.995% purity):
Bulk/Wholesale Price: ~$0.25 - $0.40 per gram. This price is derived from market reports and recent government auction data, which often report prices per thousand cubic feet (Mcf). For example, recent private sales of Grade-A helium have been reported in the range of $450-$600 per Mcf.
Retail/Lab Price: ~$1.00 - $3.50 per gram. This price is for smaller quantities, such as individual cylinders from chemical vendors, and includes significant markups for purification, packaging, and distribution.
Liquid Helium:
Bulk/Wholesale Price: ~$0.30 - $0.55 per gram. Liquid helium is more expensive than gaseous helium due to the energy-intensive liquefaction process and specialized cryogenic transportation and storage requirements. Prices for bulk liquid helium can range from $500 to over $750 per Mcf equivalent.
Retail/Dewar Price: ~$2.00 - $6.00 per gram. This is the price for smaller quantities, such as filling a dewar for laboratory use, and can be highly variable based on location and supplier.
1.2. Historical Price Data (2010-2025)
The price of helium has experienced significant volatility over the past 15 years, driven by a combination of factors including the privatization of the U.S. Federal Helium Reserve, fluctuating natural gas production (helium is a byproduct), and increasing demand from high-tech and medical sectors.
The primary source for historical pricing is the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) Minerals Yearbooks and Mineral Commodity Summaries, along with data from the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) helium auctions. 2. Noteworthy Isotope: Helium-3 (3He)
Helium-3 is a rare and highly valuable isotope with critical applications in neutron detection (for security and scientific research), cryogenics, and medical imaging. Its primary source is the decay of tritium, a component of nuclear weapons.
2.1. Helium-3 Price Analysis
Current Price (2024-2025): ~$90,000 - $100,000 per gram. The price is extremely high due to its scarcity and the critical nature of its applications. The market is largely controlled by the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Isotope Program.
Historical Price: A detailed 15-year price history for Helium-3 is not publicly available due to the small, specialized, and government-controlled nature of the market. However, reports from the early 2010s indicate prices were in the range of $1,000 - $5,000 per liter (which is significantly less than a gram). The price has skyrocketed over the last decade due to a supply crisis caused by the decommissioning of nuclear weapons (reducing tritium supply) and a surge in demand for neutron detectors for homeland security after 9/11. This is a significant data gap.

1. Lithium Price Analysis
Lithium is a soft, silvery-white alkali metal. It is not traded as a metal on a formal exchange; instead, it is typically sold as chemical compounds, primarily lithium carbonate and lithium hydroxide, under long-term contracts or on the spot market. The price is highly sensitive to demand from the battery manufacturing sector.

- **Wholesale/Bulk Price (Lithium Carbonate, Battery Grade):** ~$11,000 - $15,000 per metric ton. (This is equivalent to 81,000-110,000 CNY/T). This price is for large contracts and can be volatile.

1. Rhenium Price Analysis
Rhenium is not traded on a public exchange. It is sold as a metal powder or pellet by a small number of specialty suppliers. Its price is high and can be volatile due to its extreme rarity and dependency on molybdenum production.

1.1. Current Pricing (2024-2025)

1.2. Historical Price Data (2010-2025)

2. Noteworthy Isotopes: Stable and Medical Isotopes
Rhenium is composed of one stable and one primordial radioisotope.
- **Natural Isotopes:** ¹⁸⁵Re (37.4%, stable) and ¹⁸⁷Re (62.6%, radioactive with a half-life of 41 billion years). The decay of ¹⁸⁷Re to Osmium-187 is used in rhenium-osmium dating of ores and meteorites.

- **Pricing:** There is no commercial market for separated natural rhenium isotopes. The medical radioisotopes are produced in reactors and sold by specialized suppliers.

Technetium (Tc) Pricing and Sourcing Analysis
Report Date: August 14, 2025
This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the pricing and sourcing of Technetium (Tc), a synthetic radioactive element. It is the lightest element with no stable isotopes and is of immense importance in diagnostic nuclear medicine.

1. Technetium Price Analysis
Technetium is not a commercially traded commodity and has no "price per gram" in the traditional sense. Its value is derived from its medical applications, specifically the isotope Technetium-99m (⁹⁹ᵐTc), which is the most widely used medical radioisotope in the world.

1.1. Pricing a Synthetic Medical Isotope
The "price" of ⁹⁹ᵐTc is determined by the cost of the Technetium-99m generator (or "moly cow") from which it is obtained. These generators contain the parent isotope, Molybdenum-99 (⁹⁹Mo).
- **⁹⁹Mo Production Cost:** The primary cost driver is the production of ⁹⁹Mo in a nuclear reactor. A 2008 report by the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine estimated the production cost to be between **$125 and $325 per 6-day curie** of ⁹⁹Mo.
- **Generator Price:** Hospitals purchase ⁹⁹Mo/⁹⁹ᵐTc generators from radiopharmaceutical companies. The price depends on the total activity (in Curies) of ⁹⁹Mo in the generator at the time of calibration. This cost covers reactor irradiation, processing, generator manufacturing, and logistics.

1.2. Historical Price Data
There is no public historical price chart for technetium. The cost of ⁹⁹Mo/⁹⁹ᵐTc generators has been subject to supply shocks due to the aging and shutdown of the small number of nuclear reactors responsible for global production.

2. Noteworthy Isotopes: Medical Radioisotopes
All isotopes of technetium are radioactive.
- **Primary Medical Isotope:** ⁹⁹ᵐTc (half-life 6 hours) is ideal for diagnostic imaging. Its short half-life minimizes the radiation dose to the patient, and it emits a 140 keV gamma ray that is easily detected by SPECT cameras.
- **Parent Isotope:** ⁹⁹Mo (half-life 66 hours) is the source of ⁹⁹ᵐTc.
- **Long-Lived Isotope:** ⁹⁹Tc (half-life 211,000 years) is a fission product and is considered nuclear waste.
